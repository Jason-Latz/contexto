The Morning Market

Every morning the small town wakes to the sound of the market. A farmer brings fresh bread and cheese, and a young woman sells flowers near the old church. The water in the fountain is cold, and children run across the square while their mothers buy fruit and vegetables for the week. A teacher walks to school with a heavy book under his arm.

The baker opens his door before the sun rises. He works with flour and salt, and the warm smell of the oven fills the street. A friend stops to talk about the weather, the news, and the price of coffee. The dog of the house sleeps near the wall, happy and quiet, while a cat watches a bird on the roof of the building.

By noon the square is full of people. A doctor reads a letter from the city, a lawyer argues about a contract, and an old sailor tells a long story about the sea. The river runs slowly under the bridge, and a painter tries to capture the light on the water with a brush and a small canvas. Nothing here is in a hurry.

When the evening comes, the families return to their houses. They cook dinner, share wine, and talk about their plans for tomorrow. The night is calm and the stars are bright above the mountains. A musician plays a slow song, and the whole street listens in silence. It is a simple life, but it is a good one.
